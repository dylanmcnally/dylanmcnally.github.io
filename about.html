Identity Matrix

Often people define themselves by what they do, how they act upon the environment (ie teacher, engineering major, etc). My philosophy of identity is more complex than that of a matrix operator acting upon its environment. I refuse to limit my identity to the various roles I take.

Dylan Root-Mean-Square

I was born in Albuquerque, NM. Shortly after, my parents, older brother, and I moved to Portland, OR for my Dad's school. After that, we moved to Northern Colorado, and I lived in Loveland until graduating from Loveland High School. I owe all of my success to my parents, Tom and Jody. My mom, Jody, is the most selfless person I have ever met. The amount of time and effort that she has gave to my older brother, younger sister and I is beyond what we deserve. Her passion for kids inspires me to establish an environment, especially in academics, so that those who follow can have an amazing eperience. My dad is an absolute goof, but a respected and capable man. His passion for helping the community escapes my understanding. As a Physician Assistant in emergency medicine, he not only saves lives, but is able to connect with anyone and everyone in a manner that is qutie special.

I begin by writing about my parents because I am the person I am today because of them. I cannot thank them enough for the skills that they gave me, whcih have allowed me to evolve into a person that I can be proud of.

Spending most of my life in the Front Range of Colorado, I was fortunate enough to grow an appreciation, more of a passion, for the outdoors. To say that I enjoy the outdoors would be a gross understatement. My activities of choice include mountain biking, hiking, camping, backpacking, canoeing, snowshoeing, and hammocking.

With a passion for opting outside comes a deep respect for the environment and a desire to see it healthy. I am lucky to attend university in Boulder, CO with the multitude of bike paths and public transit options that I can cut down on my carbon footprint.
